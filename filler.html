The Problem with Most Free PDF Fillers

Most so-called free tools hide serious problems. You upload your private document, wait for processing on their servers, then face a paywall or forced sign-up before you can download your own file back. Worse, sensitive documents — tax records, visa forms, medical papers — sit on a stranger's server with no real deletion guarantee.

  • Server-side risks: Your private data travels to external servers you cannot control.
  • Signup traps: Many tools block download until you create a paid account.
  • Watermarks: Free tiers stamp your document, making it unusable for official submission.
  • Page limits: Some tools only allow 1–2 pages on the free plan.

The Helping PDF Approach: 100% Local, 100% Free

Pro Tips for Filling PDFs Professionally

Match font size to the form field. If filling a small checkbox area, use 10px or 12px so text fits naturally inside the field boundary.

Use black text for official documents. Most legal and government forms expect black ink. Keep color on #000000 unless specified otherwise.

Drag text boxes to reposition. If your text lands slightly off-target, drag it to the exact spot — no need to delete and retype.

Review every page before downloading. Use Prev and Next buttons to check all pages. Once downloaded, text is permanently embedded.
